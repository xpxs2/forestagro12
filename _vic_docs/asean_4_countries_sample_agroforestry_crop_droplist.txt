a comprehensive list of 20 smallholder-friendly agroforestry crops for each trial country, with regional notes highlighting their fit for greenbond livelihood programs.
not all provinces in each country can sustain all the crops listed.

Thailand (TH)

    Rubber (Hevea brasiliensis): South/Northeast—main tree crop, with intercrops for early income.

Pineapple (Ananas comosus): Common in small plots for local and export markets.

Rice (Oryza sativa): Planted in wet areas; staple with intercropping.

Maize (Zea mays): Dry uplands, rotation with legumes.

Chili (Capsicum spp.): Cash crop, often grown in open patches.

Long bean (Vigna unguiculata): Vegetable intercrop.

Mango (Mangifera indica): All regions; intercropped with timber.

Guava (Psidium guajava): Popular for fruit and juice.

Durian (Durio zibethinus): Eastern orchards.

Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana): Southern Thailand—shade and fruit.

Jackfruit (Artocarpus heterophyllus): Intercropped for home use and market.

Coconut (Cocos nucifera): Coastal and southern areas.

Sweet potato (Ipomoea batatas): Quick yield, soil improvement.

Cassava (Manihot esculenta): Northeast plateau—major cash root.

Salacca (Salacca zalacca): Moist lowlands.

Ironwood (Hopea ferrea): Shade/timber species in mixed plots.

Pak riang (Sauropus androgynus): Indigenous vegetable, home/market sales.

Bamboo (Dendrocalamus spp.): Used for income, construction, and shading.

Mushroom (multiple edible species): Grown in shaded tree mixes.

Gnetum (Gnetum gnemon): Edible seeds/leaves.

Lao PDR (LA)

    Teak (Tectona grandis): North/central uplands; timber and shade.

    Rubber (Hevea brasiliensis): Expansion in north/central—timber and latex.

Banana (Musa spp.): Uplands and valleys for local food/market.

Citrus (Citrus spp.): Oranges, lemons, limes—perennial fruit.

Pineapple (Ananas comosus): Upland fruit cash crop.

Maize (Zea mays): Grown in rotation with legumes.

Cassava (Manihot esculenta): Upland cash root—soil structure.

Coffee (Coffea spp.): Shade-grown in Bolaven Plateau and uplands.

Cardamom (Amomum spp.): Forest intercropping spice.

Mango (Mangifera indica): Mid-elevation plots.

Rice (Oryza sativa): Wet valley smallholder paddy.

Sweet potato (Ipomoea batatas): Upland food crop.

Ginger (Zingiber officinale): Small plot spice, intercropped.

Mulberry (Morus alba): Used for silk and leaf food crops.

Macadamia (Macadamia integrifolia): Nuts, market expansion.

Jatropha (Jatropha curcas): Bioenergy, low-maintenance.

Lemongrass (Cymbopogon citratus): Spice/herbal market.

    Soybean (Glycine max): Legume; soil restoration.

    Peanut (Arachis hypogaea): Legume intercrop.

    Papaya (Carica papaya): Fast income tropical fruit.

Indonesia (ID)

    Rubber (Hevea brasiliensis): Key agroforestry tree in Sumatra/Kalimantan.

Oil palm (Elaeis guineensis): Sumatra/Kalimantan, with diversification.

Cocoa (Theobroma cacao): Sulawesi/West Papua, long-term income.

Coffee (Coffea spp.): Java/Sulawesi, intercropped shade.

Clove (Syzygium aromaticum): Maluku/Sulawesi—spice trade.

Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans): Maluku—spice, high market value.

Cassava (Manihot esculenta): Smallholder staple, uplands.

Banana (Musa spp.): National staple and market income.

Coconut (Cocos nucifera): Coastal/lowland agroforestry.

Pepper (Piper nigrum): Lampung/Kalimantan—export crop.

Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum): West Sumatra, market spice.

Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana): Java-Bali—fruit.

Sago palm (Metroxylon sagu): Eastern lowlands—local food.

Betel nut (Areca catechu): Intercropped with timber.

Petai (Parkia speciosa): Timber/vegetable integration.

Nilam (Pogostemon cablin): Essential oil markets.

Papaya (Carica papaya): Quick, high-value fruit.

Pineapple (Ananas comosus): Sumatra/Bali for income.

    Soybean (Glycine max): Legume, nutrient improvement.

    Peanut (Arachis hypogaea): Intercropped in dry fields.



Singapore (SG)

    Red amaranth (Amaranthus gangeticus): Urban farm staple.

Mustard greens (Brassica juncea): High-yield leafy.

Cai Xin (Brassica rapa): Efficient vertical crop.

Lettuce (Lactuca sativa): Hydroponic systems.

Basil (Ocimum basilicum): Culinary, pollinator.

Mint (Mentha spp.): Urban herbs.

Eggplant (Solanum melongena): Container/rooftop.

Chili (Capsicum spp.): Market/consumption.

Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum): Roof/floor pots.

Sweet potato (Ipomoea batatas): Urban and peri-urban.

Bitter gourd (Momordica charantia): Vertical farming.

Spinach (Spinacia oleracea): Hydroponics.

Corn (Zea mays): Experiment sites; not staple.

Bok choy (Brassica rapa): Vertical garden, rapid yield.

Okra (Abelmoschus esculentus): Community plots.

Chinese broccoli (Brassica alboglabra): Popular, dense.

Spring onions (Allium fistulosum): Container growth.

Pumpkin (Cucurbita pepo): Urban edge, high value.

    Passion fruit (Passiflora edulis): Vines for vertical plots.

    Water spinach (Ipomoea aquatica): Wet hydroponics.

These crops support subsistence, local market access, and ecosystem improvement, aligning well with smallholder realities and greenbond criteria for climate, biodiversity, and income resilience.
